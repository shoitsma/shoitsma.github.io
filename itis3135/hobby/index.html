Sarah's Overwatch Journey

Introduction

Greetings! Overwatch is a competitive first-person shooter. The playable characters- known as heroes- are divided into three roles- tank, dps, and support. The responsibilities of each role are complicated but distinct. Simplified, the roles are explained as tanks being expected to take space and peel for their team, dps being expected to exert constant pressure and confirm kills, and supports being expected to heal their team as well as use their kits to enable their team.

Prompt: Winston from Overwatch

About Me

I started playing Overwatch in 2023. I played the game because I thought Wrecking Ball looked fun. He was my first main and I played him for under 60 hours before retiring him forever. My current mains are D.va, Junker Queen, Ashe, Widowmaker, Ana, and Kiriko.

Why I Play

I don't always find the game fun. Some teams are very frustrating to play against and some games feel unwinnable. Most of the time I play to improve. I try to look at every setback and think about how to learn from it. In the future I hope to achieve master rank on tank and then support followed by dps. My ultimate goal is to reach grandmaster in possibly all roles.

The Ranked Experience

The ranking system in Overwatch goes like this: bronze → silver → gold → platinum → diamond → master → grandmaster → champion. The lowest rank is bronze and the highest is the recently added champion rank. The majority of the player base is in gold/platinum. There are five subdivisions within each rank, with 5 being the lowest and 1 being the highest within that rank.

My first ever tank placement was gold 1 and I finished the end of the season at platinum 5. This was a huge milestone for me because I had officially become a platinum tank player. I placed platinum 3 on dps and gold 2 on support. For the first 400 hours of my playing the game I was a platinum player. I then reached another huge milestone on tank when I hit diamond 5. I had officially become a diamond tank player. I then pursued diamond on other roles. Somewhere between 400-600 hours I became a diamond support player and finally a diamond dps player. I came close to master rank on support at diamond 1 but then a rank reset occured and I have to work my way up from platinum again.

How I Improved

  • I chose a few heroes from each role to practice and got familiar with their kits as well as how to play them into different team compositions
  • I played in higher ranked lobbies so that my mistakes were more likely to be punished and I learned not to repeat them
  • I played the game for at least a few hours a day everyday
